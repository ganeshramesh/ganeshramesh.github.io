About Me Research Links Misc

I am a serial data scientist/engineer. I currently work at Apple with the Maps Search team. I lead some of the efforts that lie in the intersection of Machine Learning, Information Retrieval, Search and Recommender Systems with an emphasis on Representation Learning (Think Embeddings, Distributed Word Representations) with location context.

In the past I have worked at several companies dabbling in various areas of Data Management and Mining - in the areas of advertising (Amazon, Yahoo), search (Microsoft), spam detection (Microsoft), product data science (LinkedIn) and search/recommender systems for Education (Edmodo). Feel free to check out my LinkedIn profile to know more about me.

I love to stay curious and learn new things. When not solving problems, I read, listen to music, travel with my wonderful family and spend some quality time experimenting with my Fujifilm X-S10 and a 16mm f1.4 prime lens.

I have a strong bias towards applied research. I derive satisfaction in seeing things work - think code and prototypes over theorems that remain theorems. Having said that, I do believe in having a strong theoretical foundation and a solid intuitive basis for any applied work. For the past two years (until June 2020), I led and shipped the Machine learned Ranker for Address Search for Apple Maps. The ranker is responsible for serving the top ranked results for address queries within the Apple Maps app across all the devices. Millions of queries each day are impacted and served by this ranker across the world. My current work revolves around architecting applications of Machine Learning in the Search Stack for Apple Maps. Examples of specific problems are applications of Machine Translation to enhance Search Quality, Representation Learning for Maps with location context, Application of Knowledge bases to Maps Search and Neural IR.

I earned my Ph.D in Computer Science from The University at Albany (SUNY) after which I spent two wonderful years at The University of British Columbia in Vancouver. A (rather dated) list of publications from the past can be found here.

A question I ask before writing papers - "Will people devote their precious time in reading this work and benefit from it?" - If I have doubts, then I let it pass. Probably a lot of missed opportunity in getting more publications but it helps me regain a lot of time. The learnings from the work do stay back as experience - so not a total loss.
